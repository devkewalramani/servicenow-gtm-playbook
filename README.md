## Engagement Case Studies

### Paul Hastings — ITSM Transformation & ISO 27001 Compliance
**Computacenter · 2021 · Legal Sector**

| Detail | Value |
|---|---|
| Total Contract Value | $3.2M |
| Deal Type | Largest ServiceNow XS deal in Computacenter US history |
| Partner | Thirdera (ServiceNow Elite SI) |
| Win Date | December 2021 |

**Client Objective:** Paul Hastings had a strategic mandate to certify as ISO 27001 compliant within 2 to 3 years. To achieve that, they needed to define and implement an ITSM Operating Model and associated ServiceNow tooling.

**Scope of Work:**
- Defined and documented a Target Services Operating Model
- Developed a ServiceNow roadmap and implementation timeline
- Delivered ITSM modules, workflows, integrations, and process documentation
- Provided flexible ServiceNow Managed Services capabilities (Admin, Change, Upgrade)
- Provided ITSM Program leadership, project management, and governance resources

**Commercial Motion:**
- Relationship-led sale built on proven delivery credibility
- Multi-year ITSM program structure with Day 2 Managed Services embedded
- Partner-coordinated delivery with Thirdera as the ServiceNow delivery partner
- Required alignment across IT leadership, procurement, and legal operations

**Key Lesson for Partner GTM:** The sale was won on the strength of the partner relationship with Thirdera and on depth of delivery credibility — not on price. ServiceNow enterprise sales at this size require the partner to carry both the commercial conversation and the delivery confidence simultaneously. Separating those two motions costs deals.

## Partner Channel Principles

Based on ten years across these four engagement angles, here are the principles that consistently drive successful ServiceNow partner GTM:

**1. The warmest pipeline is always inside an existing delivery relationship.**
New ServiceNow logos from cold outreach close slowly and at lower ACV. The highest-conversion motion is identifying ServiceNow whitespace inside accounts where you already have delivery credibility.

**2. Product and services must be bundled from the first conversation.**
Customers who buy product without services rarely achieve the outcomes they expected. Partners who separate the two motions create churn. Services attach at the point of sale is a commercial discipline, not an upsell.

**3. The platform is bigger than ITSM.**
ITSM is the entry point. The expansion opportunity is in CMDB, ITOM, PPM, custom app development, and AI-driven automation. Partners who only sell ITSM leave most of the platform value — and most of the revenue — on the table.

**4. Day 2 is the moat.**
Managed services, upgrade support, and ongoing optimization are where partner economics actually work. The initial implementation is the entry cost. The managed services relationship is the asset.

**5. The buyer has changed.**
ServiceNow decisions increasingly involve business unit leaders, not just IT. The CHRO, CFO, and COO are now in the room. Partner sellers who can hold that conversation — connecting platform capability to business outcomes in non-technical language — win at a different rate than those who can only speak to IT leadership.

**6. AI is reshaping the GTM motion right now.**
ServiceNow's Now Assist and AI Agents roadmap is giving partners a new entry point into accounts that have stalled on traditional ITSM expansion. The AI conversation opens doors that the platform renewal conversation does not.
